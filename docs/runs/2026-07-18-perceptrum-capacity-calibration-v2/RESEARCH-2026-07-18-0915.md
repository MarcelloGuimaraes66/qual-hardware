# Research — Perceptrum capacity calibration v2

## Problem and alternatives

1. Exact-machine-only benchmarks are safest but impractical because only 3–4 machines are available.
2. A single composite public score is simple but unsafe: RTSP decode, image processing, local inference, disk and thermals scale differently.
3. Stage-specific proportional extrapolation calibrated by physical anchors is auditable and matches the available evidence. This is selected.

## Selected method

- Normalize every public observation as a larger-is-better score for one stage and one versioned profile.
- For anchor `a`, target `t`, stage `s`: `raw(t,s,a)=measured(a,s)*public(t,s)/public(a,s)`.
- Combine anchors with the minimum prediction, then apply empirical correction and a reserve: A 20%, B 30%, C 40%.
- Overall camera capacity is the minimum stage capacity. Never average upward and never call extrapolated hardware physically validated.
- Leave-one-out checks all available anchors; unsafe overestimation demotes the target or increases reserve.

## Evidence policy

- Tier 1: manufacturer specifications and versioned standardized results (Intel/AMD/NVIDIA/Apple, MLCommons, OpenBenchmarking, Blender Open Data, SPEC subject to its use rules).
- Tier 2: exact configuration/version benchmark browsers.
- Tier 3: curated independent tests with disclosed power, cooling, OS and driver.
- Reject cross-version scores, anonymous/overclocked results and marketing-only claims.
- Deliver signed offline snapshots; do not scrape websites from the desktop application.

## Compatibility

The selected design extends existing JSON records and SQLite v1 rather than replacing them. Legacy manifest/result routes remain readable while the UI and new routes use offline plan/result files. External memory/Obsidian was unavailable; project-local Archon artifacts are the continuity source.
